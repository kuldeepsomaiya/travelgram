Paragliding

Paragliding is the recreational and competitive adventure sport of flying paragliders: lightweight, free-flying, foot-launched glider aircraft with no rigid primary structure.[1] The pilot sits in a harness suspended below a fabric wing. Wing shape is maintained by the suspension lines, the pressure of air entering vents in the front of the wing, and the aerodynamic forces of the air flowing over the outside.As opposed to traditional non-motorized paragliders you can launch from level ground under your own power, flying as you please. Powered paraglider allows you to be as close to a bird as it is humanly possible.

Rock climbing

Rock climbing is a sport that makes you climb massive rocks, testing your endurance levels as it requires certain standards of fitness and acts as a good body workout. Red River Gorge in Kentucky encompasses the greatest concentration of arches and diverse routes. The variety of routes, superb weather, and year round climbing at Kalymnos, Greece, make it one of the best rock climbing destinations in the world. The list of the world’s best rock climbing locations is not complete without Yosemite National Park in California, which is meant for experienced climbers.

Trekking

Trekking is the most common adventure sports worldwide. The diverse topography available all over the world, with hillocks, plateaus, mountains, and caves, makes trekking one of the most liked adventure sports. Routeburn Track in New Zealand, a 20 miles long trail, passes through two diverse and awe-inspiring national parks, namely Mt. Aspiring and Fiordland. Trekking here offers stunning views of splendid landscapes, peaks, water bodies, and the superb spectacle of wild birds. Everest Base Camp in Nepal, The Inca Trail in Peru, and The Haute Route, France-Switzerland are also a few of the enchanted routes with picturesque views..
